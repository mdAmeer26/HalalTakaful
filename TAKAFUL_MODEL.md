## â“ Frequently Asked Questions

### Q1: Is this really different from insurance?
**Yes!** Key differences:

| Aspect | Conventional Insurance | Islamic Takaful |
|--------|----------------------|-----------------|
| **Nature** | Risk transfer (you sell risk) | Mutual cooperation (risk sharing) |
| **Payment** | Premium (purchase) | Tabarru (donation) |
| **Ownership** | Company owns all premiums | Pool owned collectively by participants |
| **Profit** | Company keeps all profits | Surplus shared with participants |
| **Investments** | May include interest, haram stocks | Only Shariah-compliant investments |
| **Gharar** | May have hidden terms | Fully transparent |

### Q2: What if the pool runs out of money?
- Platform provides **Qard Hasan** (interest-free loan) to the pool
- Loan is repaid when pool has surplus
- Participants may be asked for additional voluntary contributions
- Platform does NOT take interest on this loan

### Q3: How do you ensure investments are Halal?
- Dedicated **Shariah Advisory Board** of qualified scholars
- Regular audits of all investments
- Use of Shariah-compliant screening tools
- Public disclosure of investment portfolios

### Q4: Can I get a refund if I cancel?
- Unlike insurance, Tabarru is a **donation** - generally non-refundable
- However, any **savings component** (in investment-linked plans) can be returned
- Wakalah fees are non-refundable (already used for services)

### Q5: What happens to surplus in the pool?
Options approved by Shariah scholars:
1. **Distribute to participants** proportionally
2. **Lower future contributions**
3. **Donate to charity** (with participant consent)
4. **Keep as reserves** for future claims
